Social Media Best Practices

Platform-specific best practices:

  • LinkedIn: Professional tone, industry insights, company updates, longer text format (1300 characters max in feed), business audience, weekdays during business hours
  • Twitter/X: Concise (280 characters), timely content, multiple posts per day, hashtags (1-2), conversational tone, questions, polls
  • Facebook: Mix of media types, community building, personal stories, moderate post frequency, video prioritized by algorithm
  • Instagram: High-quality visuals, up to 30 hashtags, stories for time-sensitive content, carousel posts for education, grid aesthetics
  • TikTok: Short entertaining videos, authentic tone, trending sounds/challenges, educational content, younger audience
  • YouTube: Detailed video scripts, search-optimized titles/descriptions, calls for subscription, longer content format
  • Pinterest: Vertical visuals (2:3 ratio), keyword-rich descriptions, seasonal content, inspirational/educational focus
  • Threads: Conversational, text-focused, community building, minimal hashtags, authentic voice

Content type strategies:

  • Text Post: Clear value, concise phrasing, one main idea, relevant emoji usage, proper formatting
  • Image Post: High-quality visuals, text overlay for key points, brand consistency, emotional connection
  • Carousel: Progressive story/information flow, numbered indicators, complete message in first slide, cohesive design
  • Video Script: Hook in first 3 seconds, clear structure, captions for accessibility, optimal platform length
  • Story: Vertical format, interactive elements (polls, questions), urgency, personality, behind-the-scenes content
  • Poll: Clear question, balanced options, follow-up engagement plan, conversation starter
  • Event: Complete details (what, when, where), clear value proposition, registration steps, reminders
  • Job Posting: Role highlights, company culture, clear requirements, application instructions, employee testimonials
  • Product Launch: Problem-solution format, key features, social proof, limited-time offers, next steps
  • Company Update: Narrative structure, human element, future vision, community impact, celebrations

Engagement optimization:

  • Include a question to encourage comments
  • Use the "hook, story, offer" framework for compelling content
  • Incorporate timely or trending topics when relevant
  • Share authentic behind-the-scenes content or personal anecdotes
  • Create content that sparks emotion (inspiration, surprise, curiosity)
  • Use power words that trigger emotional responses
  • Highlight community successes and user-generated content
  • Develop content themes or series to build anticipation

Visual elements:

  • Use high-contrast, eye-catching imagery that stops the scroll
  • Maintain consistent brand colors, fonts, and visual identity
  • Include human faces to increase engagement
  • Ensure text overlays are legible (recommended 20% text to 80% image ratio)
  • Use motion (video, GIFs, animations) to increase attention
  • Consider accessibility: alt text, captions, color contrast
  • Follow platform-specific image dimensions and specifications
  • Create custom graphics for quotes, statistics, and key messages

Hashtag best practices:

  • Popular hashtags: High reach potential but more competition (#marketing, #business)
  • Niche hashtags: More targeted, better engagement rate (#contentmarketingstrategy)
  • Branded hashtags: Build recognition and collect UGC (#yourbrandname)
  • Trending hashtags: Temporarily boosted visibility if relevant to your content
  • Platform recommendations:
    • LinkedIn: 3-5 relevant hashtags
    • Twitter: 1-2 strategic hashtags
    • Instagram: Up to 30 (mix of sizes, often in first comment)
    • Facebook: Minimal (1-2) or none
    • TikTok: Mix of trending and niche (3-5)

Call-to-action strategies:

  • Use clear, direct action verbs (Click, Download, Share, Comment)
  • Create urgency with time-limited offers
  • Make the next step obvious and easy to complete
  • Ask directly for the engagement you want ("Double tap if you agree")
  • Use one primary CTA per post (avoid multiple competing actions)
  • Test different CTA placements (beginning, middle, end)
  • Provide a compelling reason WHY they should take action
  • Consider using directional cues pointing to action buttons

Timing strategies:

  • Schedule content when your audience is most active (check analytics)
  • Consider time zones if targeting global audiences
  • Post 30-60 minutes before peak activity times
  • Test different posting times and analyze performance
  • Adjust timing based on day of week (weekdays vs. weekends)
  • Plan around industry events or relevant holidays
  • Consider posting during commuting hours for professional content
  • Be ready to engage and respond within the first hour after posting
